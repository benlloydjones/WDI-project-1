Welcome to Bacchus

The aim of the game is to capture seven of your opponent's counters. You can capture one of your opponent's pieces by having three of your counters in a horizontal or vertical line (as indicated by the lines on the board), this is known as a mill.

The game has two phases:

  1. In this phase you place your counters, in turn, on the board, you have nine counters to place.
  2. In this phase you move your counters to an adjacent point (as indicated by the lines on the board).

You can break a mill by moving your counter out of it and then in your next turn reform the mill, allowing you to take another of your opponent's counters

When capturing your opponent's counters, you can only remove counters that are not in a mill, unless, all of your opponent's counters are in mills.

Directions over what your next action is can be seen to the left of the board.
